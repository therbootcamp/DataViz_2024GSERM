Details

Element Details
Pitches (i) In this session, you will introduce your visualization sketch to the rest of the group. You will have 2 slides and 2 minutes for your pitch. Slide 1 should only show a project title and an image (e.g., from unsplash.com.) that helps you introduce and motivate your research question. Slide 2 should only show your hand-drawn visualization sketch and a slide title. No bullet points allowed. Be aware that 2 minutes pass by quickly. Please practice and ideally time your presentation beforehand.

(ii) After each presentation there will be 3 minutes for peer feedback, so please be prepared to comment on fellow participants’ presentations. We will provide our feedback individually via Slack after the session.

(iii) To ensure a swift transition between presentations, we ask you to submit your pitch presentation slides until Monday 11:59pm via this Google form.
Project work (i) In the project work session, you have the opportunity to book 15 minute one-on-one consulting slots with us to go over roadblocks in your code or discuss design aspects of your visualization. To book your session, please enter your name here and then check in the relevant breakout room (Track 1 or Track 2) in the regular Zoom session at the appropriate time. If you are not facing roadblocks or have a design question, please nevertheless book a slot to update us on your progress.
Final presentation (i) In this session, you will communicate the results of your research question using your final visualization. As for the pitch, you will have 2 slides—an image-based intro slide and a slide for your final visualization—but you will this time have 3 minutes for your presentation. Again, no bullet points or text besides titles. Practice and time your presentation beforehand. This is your moment to shine–tell a good story using your compelling data visualization!

(ii) To ensure a swift transition between presentations, we ask you to submit your final presentation slides until Thursday 11:59pm via this Google form.
Final report (i) The final assignment of this course is to compile and submit a one-to-two-page report. The report should include a (i) catchy title and abstract (max 50 words), (ii) text presenting the research question, the methodology and data, the results, and conclusions, and, of course, (iii) the final data visualization. The report should be created using R markdown and submitted until Friday, July 15th, 11:59pm as a self-contained html file via this Google form. The final report needs to be submitted only by those who wish to obtain a certificate with a grade.
